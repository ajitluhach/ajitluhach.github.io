The Ballad of Buster Scruggs by Coen Brothers

Posted on Fri 11 January 2019 in reviews • 1 min read

It's a anthology series of 6 poems written, created, and directed by the Coen Brothers. They devised the poems for 25 years with an intent to create a mini series with 6 chapters. Inventually, the mini series was shrunken down to a single movie of 2hours+ total playtime. Every character in the story is brilliantly created, their arc brilliantly told. The movie starts out with The Ballad of Buster Scruggs, where Buster Scruggs, also famously known in the west as San Saba Songbird is played by Tim Blake. He is a gunslinger who likes to sing, his gun is fast and his words provocative but there's always someone far more quicker than he is. The second chapter is Near Algodones, James Franco plays a cowboy who is about to rob a bank, only to be caught by the old manager of the bank.
